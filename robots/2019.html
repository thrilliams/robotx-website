FRC 2019 Deep Space

In 2019’s Deep Space FRC season, RobotX began the season with a year of experience, twice the team members, two more mentors, and still barely an idea in our collective heads. The first 2 weeks were spent designing, calculating, realizing the calculations were wrong, and redesigning. After the 2.5 week mark, we decided that we’ve stalled enough and it was time to start building. The general game plan before we saw other teams was to fill up the rocket with hatches. As we got to the competition, we slowly realized that that was the exact opposite of the correct game plan. Thankfully, we made our robot adaptable, such that we can take on almost any role. We fought our way through our qualifying matches, ranking anywhere from 5th to 25th. We ended the qualifiers in 16th place overall, making it likely that a top 8 team would pick us. And they did! On the 5th seed alliance, we were the only lower-seed team to win a match in the quarterfinals. This year, we took home the Team Spirit award, and we’re looking for more next year!

About the Robot

ARM2-D2 was purpose-built by our team as a hybrid offense bot. The design was highly reminiscent of our 2018 robot, albeit with a significantly longer arm. It used intake wheels to grab and maneuver the cargo and had a hook to pick up and put down hatch panels. The design, although flawed in some areas, performed extremely well overall.

CalGames

Our resounding success in the San Francisco regionals left our team hungry for more, so we participated in WRRF's 2019 CalGames. Our team members gave it their all, and it paid off. We ended up going eight and four, landing us the rank of fifth overall team. Although we made it to the quarterfinals, we lost in the first match after a tie in the first round.

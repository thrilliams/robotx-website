FRC 2018 Power Up!

The field was set up with 3 alliance members on both sides of the field, the team's robot ready to play. The match would start off with a 30 second autonomous periods where teams could pick up power cubes and place them in the switch or scale (the switch and scales are like seesaws, you would put power cubes on them to tip the scales in your favor and get points). The game starts with the autonomous period and then the manual period would start. The alliances would get power cubes and either put them on the scale, switch, or in the power ups section; This was a station where the robot put boxes in and the teams decided which power ups they wanted, there were 3 power ups to choose from, they were, levitate, control, and boost where each would do different things to help a team during the game. As the match came to a close, the alliances would then attempt to go to the middle of the field and then lift themselves at least 2 feet off the ground by hanging on a bar. This would also get the alliance points. After that, the game would come to a close as the fields were cleared off and getting ready for the next game.

About the Robot

Red EVA was tactfully designed by our team to manuver milk crates. We decided to use an arm design becuase of its robustness and its smiplicity to build. Our robot was easily servicable and was efficient at completing a multitude of jobs manuvering milk crates efficiently near the ground. In order to manipulate the game pieces, we have a gripper that uses compliance wheels to intake the milk crate and can hold it while in the air. This design has been very efficient and works on a variety of different objects.
